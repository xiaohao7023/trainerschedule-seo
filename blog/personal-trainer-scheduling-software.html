Best Scheduling Software for Personal Trainers in 2026

June 2026 • 8 min read

You're a personal trainer with 20 clients, 3 different package types, and a WhatsApp inbox full of "Can we move Tuesday to Wednesday?" messages. You need scheduling software that actually understands how independent trainers work — not enterprise gym management tools that cost $200/month and require a PhD to set up.

We tested 5 popular options and compared them on what matters: session tracking, package management, pricing, and how fast you can get started.

What Independent Trainers Actually Need

Before comparing apps, let's be honest about what solo trainers need vs. what enterprise platforms sell you:

  • Session packages — Track how many sessions each client has left in their package, and auto-deduct when a session is completed
  • Calendar view — See your week at a glance with booked, completed, and pending sessions
  • Client database — Store client info, contact details, and session history in one place
  • Reminders — Get notified before sessions so you don't forget
  • Client health alerts — Know which clients are running low on sessions or haven't booked in weeks
  • Price — Something that doesn't eat your profit margin

Top 5 Scheduling Apps for Personal Trainers

1. TrainerSchedule — Best for Independent Trainers Who Want Simplicity

TrainerSchedule is a native iOS app built specifically for freelance trainers. No account required — download, add your clients, and start scheduling.

  • Session packages — Create packages with course type (e.g., "PT 1-on-1", "Group HIIT"), total sessions, and unit price. Sessions auto-deduct when you mark a booking complete.
  • Client Health monitoring — The Sessions tab shows you which clients are running low (≤2 sessions left), which have empty packages, and which haven't trained in 21+ days.
  • Weekly trend chart — Visual breakdown of completed sessions over the last 7 days.
  • iCloud sync — Pro users get automatic iCloud backup across devices. Free tier stores data locally.
  • Smart reminders — Two notifications per session: 1 hour before (prepare) and 10 minutes after start (mark complete).
  • 100% private — Data lives on your device. No servers, no accounts, no third-party access.

💡 Best for: Trainers with 1-50 clients who want to track session packages without monthly fees. Free for up to 5 members. Pro ($29.99/year) unlocks unlimited members and iCloud sync.

2. Trainerize — Best All-in-One Platform (But Expensive)

Trainerize is a full-featured platform with workout programming, nutrition tracking, and client communication built in. Great if you need everything — but most solo trainers don't.

  • Pros: Workout builder, nutrition plans, client app, payment processing
  • Cons: Starts at $5/month per active client (adds up fast), steep learning curve, requires clients to download a separate app
  • Reality check: A trainer with 20 clients pays $100+/month. Most features go unused by solo trainers.

3. TrueCoach — Best for Online/Remote Training

TrueCoach focuses on programming and video feedback, making it ideal for trainers who work remotely or run online coaching programs.

  • Pros: Video messaging, workout programming, progress tracking
  • Cons: Limited scheduling features, no session package tracking, no calendar view for in-person sessions
  • Price: $5-$30/month depending on client count

4. My PT Hub — Best for Small Gyms

My PT Hub offers scheduling, booking, and payment processing. Designed for small gyms and studios rather than individual trainers.

  • Pros: Online booking, payment processing, class scheduling
  • Cons: Overkill for solo trainers, monthly fee, no session package tracking
  • Price: $25-$100/month

5. Google Calendar — Best Free Option (If You Don't Need Packages)

If you just need basic scheduling and don't sell session packages, Google Calendar works fine. But you'll end up managing session counts in a spreadsheet.

  • Pros: Free, cross-platform, familiar interface
  • Cons: No session tracking, no client database, no packages, no reminders for session completion

How to Choose the Right App

Ask yourself these questions:

  1. Do you sell session packages? If yes, TrainerSchedule (built-in package tracking) or Google Calendar + spreadsheet (manual). If no, Google Calendar might be enough.
  2. How many clients do you have? Under 50? TrainerSchedule. Over 50? Trainerize or My PT Hub.
  3. Do you need workout programming? If yes, Trainerize or TrueCoach. If no, TrainerSchedule.
  4. Is privacy important? If yes, TrainerSchedule (100% on-device, no account required). If no, any cloud-based option.
  5. What's your budget? Free? TrainerSchedule (up to 5 clients) or Google Calendar. Paid? TrainerSchedule Pro ($2.50/month) vs. Trainerize ($100+/month).

Final Thoughts

The best scheduling software is the one that matches how you actually work. If you're an independent trainer who sells session packages and wants to track client health without paying $100+/month, TrainerSchedule is worth trying. If you need workout programming or online booking, Trainerize or My PT Hub might be better fits — just be ready for the monthly bill.

The key is to stop managing sessions in your head or in WhatsApp threads. Your clients deserve a professional experience, and your business deserves better than a spreadsheet.
