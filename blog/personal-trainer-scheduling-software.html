Best Scheduling Software for Personal Trainers in 2026

June 2026 • 7 min read

As an independent personal trainer, your schedule is your business. Missed bookings, double-bookings, and lost client information cost you money. The right scheduling software can fix all of that — but with dozens of options available, which one should you choose?

What to Look For in Trainer Scheduling Software

Before comparing apps, define what matters most to you:

  • Session tracking — Can you see how many sessions each client has left?
  • Package management — Can you create session packages and auto-deduct?
  • Client database — Can you store client info, notes, and history?
  • Calendar view — Is it easy to see your week at a glance?
  • Price — Free, freemium, or subscription?
  • Privacy — Where does your client data live?

Top 5 Scheduling Apps for Personal Trainers

1. TrainerSchedule — Best for Independent Trainers

TrainerSchedule is a lightweight iOS app built specifically for freelance trainers who want simplicity without the complexity of enterprise software.

  • Pros: No account needed, instant setup, iCloud sync, session packages, 100% private
  • Cons: iOS only, no client-facing booking page
  • Price: Free with premium features

💡 Best for: Trainers with 1-50 clients who want a simple, private way to track sessions without paying monthly fees.

2. Trainerize — Best All-in-One Platform

Trainerize is a full-featured platform with workout programming, nutrition tracking, and client communication built in.

  • Pros: Workout programming, nutrition plans, client app, payment processing
  • Cons: Expensive, steep learning curve, requires client app download
  • Price: $5-$25/month per active client

3. TrueCoach — Best for Online Training

TrueCoach focuses on programming and client communication, making it ideal for trainers who work remotely.

  • Pros: Workout builder, video messaging, progress tracking
  • Cons: Limited scheduling features, no session packages
  • Price: $5-$30/month

4. My PT Hub — Best for Small Gyms

My PT Hub offers scheduling, booking, and payment processing in one platform.

  • Pros: Online booking, payment processing, class scheduling
  • Cons: Overkill for solo trainers, monthly fee
  • Price: $25-$100/month

5. Google Calendar — Best Free Option

If you just need basic scheduling, Google Calendar is free and works everywhere.

  • Pros: Free, cross-platform, familiar interface
  • Cons: No session tracking, no client database, no packages
  • Price: Free

How to Choose the Right App

Ask yourself these questions:

  1. How many clients do you have? Under 50? TrainerSchedule or Google Calendar. Over 50? Trainerize or My PT Hub.
  2. Do you need workout programming? If yes, Trainerize or TrueCoach. If no, TrainerSchedule.
  3. Do you need online booking? If yes, My PT Hub or Trainerize. If no, TrainerSchedule.
  4. Is privacy important? If yes, TrainerSchedule (100% on-device). If no, any cloud-based option.
  5. What's your budget? Free? TrainerSchedule or Google Calendar. Paid? Trainerize or TrueCoach.
