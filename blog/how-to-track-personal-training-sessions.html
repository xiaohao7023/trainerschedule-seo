How to Track Personal Training Sessions Without Spreadsheets

June 2026 • 5 min read

Most independent personal trainers start with spreadsheets. It's free, familiar, and seems to work — until it doesn't. Here's why spreadsheets fail and what to use instead.

Why Spreadsheets Don't Work for Session Tracking

  • Manual updates — You have to remember to update after every session
  • No calendar view — You can't see your week at a glance
  • Easy to make errors — One wrong formula and your counts are off
  • No package tracking — You have to manually count remaining sessions
  • Not mobile-friendly — Updating a spreadsheet on your phone during a session is awkward
  • Privacy concerns — Client data lives in a file that could be shared accidentally

The Better Way: A Dedicated Session Tracker

A purpose-built app like TrainerSchedule solves all these problems:

1. Automatic Session Counting

Create a package (e.g., "10 sessions for $500"), assign it to a client, and the app automatically deducts sessions as you mark them complete. No manual counting.

2. Visual Calendar

See your entire week in a clean calendar view. Tap any slot to book a session. No more scrolling through spreadsheet rows.

3. Client Database

All your clients in one place — name, phone, notes, session history. Search instantly instead of Ctrl+F in Excel.

4. Works Offline

No internet? No problem. Everything works offline and syncs via iCloud when you're back online.

5. Private by Default

Your client data stays on your phone. No cloud servers, no third-party access, no risk of accidental sharing.

💡 Pro tip: If you're currently using spreadsheets, export your client list and start fresh in a dedicated app. The 10 minutes you spend setting up will save you hours every month.

How to Migrate from Spreadsheets

  1. Export your client list — Get names and phone numbers into a simple list
  2. Download TrainerSchedule — No account needed, start immediately
  3. Add your clients — Takes about 30 seconds per client
  4. Create session packages — Set up your standard packages

What to Track for Each Session

At minimum, track these for every session:

  • Client name — Who the session is with
  • Date and time — When it's scheduled
  • Package — Which package it's deducted from
  • Status — Completed, cancelled, or no-show
  • Notes — What you worked on (optional but helpful)

Final Thoughts

Spreadsheets are great for many things, but tracking personal training sessions isn't one of them. A dedicated app saves time, reduces errors, and gives you a professional way to manage your business.
