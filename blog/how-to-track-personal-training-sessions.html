How to Track Personal Training Sessions Without Spreadsheets

June 2026 • 6 min read

You sell a 12-session package to a client. Three months later, you're not sure if they've used 7 or 9 sessions. You check your spreadsheet — but you forgot to update it after last week. Now you're guessing, and that's not professional.

Sound familiar? Most independent trainers manage session tracking through a combination of memory, WhatsApp messages, and a Google Sheet that's always out of date. Here's why that breaks down, and what to do instead.

Why Spreadsheets Fail for Session Tracking

Spreadsheets work until they don't. Here's what typically goes wrong:

  • Manual updates get skipped — You finish a session, tell yourself you'll update the sheet later, and forget
  • No real-time visibility — You can't quickly see which clients are running low on sessions
  • No calendar integration — You're tracking sessions in a spreadsheet and scheduling in Google Calendar — two systems that don't talk to each other
  • No automatic deductions — Every completed session requires a manual formula update
  • Client health is invisible — You don't know a client hasn't booked in 3 weeks until they email you

A Better Way: Package-Based Session Tracking

The core idea is simple: instead of tracking sessions individually, track them as packages. Here's how it works in practice:

Step 1: Create Session Packages

When a client purchases sessions, create a package with:

  • Course type — e.g., "PT 1-on-1", "Group HIIT", "Online Check-in"
  • Total sessions — e.g., 12 sessions
  • Unit price — e.g., $80/session (optional, for your reference)

Step 2: Schedule Bookings

When a client books a session, create a booking linked to their package. The app knows which package to deduct from.

Step 3: Mark Complete → Auto-Deduct

After each session, tap "Complete" on the booking. The session count automatically decreases. No spreadsheet公式. No manual update.

Step 4: Monitor Client Health

This is where it gets powerful. A good tracking system shows you:

  • Low session alerts — Clients with ≤2 sessions remaining (time to upsell a new package)
  • Empty package alerts — Clients with 0 sessions left (need to purchase more)
  • Inactive client detection — Clients who haven't trained in 21+ days (time to follow up)

💡 Pro tip: The best time to sell a new package is when a client has 2-3 sessions remaining — not after they've run out. Client Health alerts make this automatic.

What to Look For in a Session Tracker

Not all apps handle session tracking well. Here's what to check:

  1. Package-aware booking — Can you link a booking to a specific package? If not, you're back to manual tracking.
  2. Auto-deduction — Does completing a session automatically update the package count?
  3. Remaining session visibility — Can you see remaining sessions at a glance on the member list?
  4. Usage statistics — Total sessions purchased vs. used vs. remaining, with a progress bar.
  5. Weekly trend — How many sessions did you complete this week vs. last week?
